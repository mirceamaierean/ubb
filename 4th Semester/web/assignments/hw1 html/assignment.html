History of Formula 1

Formula 1 is the highest class of single-seater auto racing sanctioned by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA).
The "formula" in the name refers to a set of rules to which all participants and cars must conform. The F1 world championship season consists of a series of races, known as Grands Prix, held usually on purpose-built circuits, and in a few cases on closed city streets

Formula 1 Teams

Team Description
Mercedes-AMG Petronas Formula One Team One of the most successful teams in Formula 1 history, Mercedes has dominated the sport in recent years with their innovative engineering and talented drivers.
Scuderia Ferrari Ferrari, the oldest and most successful team in Formula 1, holds a special place in the hearts of racing fans worldwide with its iconic red cars.
Red Bull Racing Red Bull Racing has been a major force in Formula 1 since the early 2000s, known for its aggressive driving style and innovative car designs.
McLaren F1 Team McLaren has a rich history in Formula 1, with multiple World Championships and a reputation for developing some of the fastest cars on the grid.
Alpine F1 Team Formerly known as Renault, the team has a long history in Formula 1 and has been a major player in the sport for decades.

Formula 1 Drivers

Lewis Hamilton

One of the most decorated drivers in Formula 1 history, Lewis Hamilton has won multiple World Championships and is known for his exceptional speed and skill on the track.

Max Verstappen

The young Dutch prodigy has quickly risen through the ranks to become one of the most exciting and aggressive drivers in Formula 1.

Formula 1 Races

Formula 1 races take place in various locations around the world, from iconic circuits like Monaco to modern facilities like Yas Marina Circuit in Abu Dhabi.

Engines in Formula 1

Formula 1 engines are a key component of the cars, and have evolved significantly over the years. Today, they are hybrid power units, combining a 1.6 litre V6 turbocharged engine with energy recovery systems.

Did you know?

Formula 1 engines can rev up to 15,000 rpm and produce around 1000 horsepower. However, the power units are not just about peak power, but also about how efficiently they can use fuel, and how much electrical power they can generate.
